A landslide is a natural disaster in which soil, rocks, and debris move down a slope due to gravity. Landslides usually occur in hilly or mountainous regions, especially during heavy rainfall. When the soil becomes saturated with water, it loses strength and starts sliding down. Deforestation, road construction, mining, and earthquakes can also increase the risk of landslides.
